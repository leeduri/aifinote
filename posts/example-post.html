How I Actually Use AI Tools Every Day

By Your Name Jul 19, 2026 6 min read

After a year of trying almost every AI tool that trended, only four made it into my daily routine. The rest were fun for a week and then forgotten. Here's what stuck, and — more usefully — what each one actually replaced.

The test: does it save real time?

My rule is simple. A tool earns a spot only if, after two weeks, skipping it feels slower. Novelty doesn't count. Saved minutes do.

What survived, and what it replaced

Tool typeReplacedTime saved / week
Writing assistantBlank-page procrastination~2 hrs
Code helperStack Overflow tab-hopping~3 hrs
SummarizerReading full reports~1 hr

Where AI still gets in the way

It's not all upside. For anything requiring judgment or a real point of view, AI gives me a fast draft that's confidently mediocre. The editing back to something honest often costs more than writing it myself would have.

The tools that lasted all share one trait: they remove a chore, not the thinking.

Wrapping up

If you're drowning in AI recommendations, ignore the hype cycle and run the two-week test yourself. Most tools won't pass — and that's the point.
